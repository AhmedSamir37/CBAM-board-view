1) What is this app?

CBAM Board View is a lightweight dashboard designed to estimate potential CBAM cost exposure for steel exports to the European Union.

It is intentionally simplified for board-level stress-testing, scenario planning, and quick decision support — not for formal regulatory reporting.

When to use it

  • Board discussion: “How big is our EU CBAM exposure this year?”
  • Commercial planning: “What €/t penalty could hit our export margins?”
  • Strategy: “Which lever reduces exposure fastest: intensity, EU share, exemptions, or carbon price?”

2) How to use (step-by-step)

  1. Open Inputs via Edit inputs.
  2. Enter values (or keep defaults).
  3. Press Apply to compute KPIs and scenarios.
  4. Use Copy share link to capture the exact state (inputs + scenario) in the URL.
  5. Use Reset to clear inputs and return to a clean state.
  6. Use Download CSV to export a structured snapshot (for spreadsheet work).
  7. Use Print / Save PDF to produce a board-ready one-pager.

Board tip

  • Use Base for “current state”, then click Stress to show downside risk in 5 seconds.
  • Show the board €/t exported first — it maps directly to margin impact.

3) Inputs (user enters)

Field Meaning Example
Total production (t/y) Total annual steel production (all markets). 4,500,000
EU share (%) Share of total production exported to the EU. 18
EU tons (t/y) Auto-calculated as production × EU share, but can be overridden manually. 810,000
Emissions intensity (tCO₂/t exported) Carbon intensity of exported steel (simplified single value). 2.10
Carbon price (€/tCO₂) ETS/CBAM carbon price assumption used for stress-testing. 80
Exemptions / free allocation (%) Any percentage of emissions assumed not charged (simplified lever). 0
FX EUR/USD (optional) Used only in CSV display (core KPIs remain in EUR). 1.10
Route label (text) Label for the scenario route (EAF, DRI+EAF, Mixed). EAF

Note: If you manually type EU tons, the app treats it as an override (it will not auto-update from production/share).

4) Outputs (app results)

KPI What it tells the board Simple calculation
Covered emissions (tCO₂) How many tons of CO₂ are “priced” under the simplified model. EU tons × intensity
Annual CBAM exposure (€/year) Gross annual exposure before exemptions/free allocation. tCO₂ × carbon price
Net exposure after exemptions (€/year) Exposure after applying the simplified exemption lever. Gross × (1 − exemptions)
CBAM cost per ton exported (€/t) Margin impact per exported ton — most actionable KPI. Net / EU tons

How to interpret results

  • €/t exported → maps directly to commercial margin and pricing power.
  • €/year → board-level exposure and financing/strategy relevance.
  • tCO₂ → operational decarbonization signal (intensity matters).

5) Scenarios

The app provides three scenario modes:

Mode Purpose Typical interpretation
Base Current input values. “Our best estimate / current state.”
Conservative Lower carbon price and/or improved intensity assumptions (lighter exposure). “What if conditions are favorable or we improve performance.”
Stress Higher carbon price and/or worse intensity assumptions (heavier exposure). “Downside case / risk boundary.”

Scenario multipliers are configurable in the app’s script.js (SCENARIOS object).

6) Assumptions & limitations (read before using externally)

  • This tool is a simplified decision model for stress-testing, not a compliance calculator.
  • Real CBAM reporting requires verified system boundaries, product method rules, MRV, and EU reporting formats.
  • Intensity is treated as one value, while real systems may differ by product SKU, route, and site energy mix.
  • Exemptions/free allocation are simplified as a single percentage lever.

Recommended language when sharing

  • “Indicative estimate for decision stress-testing.”
  • “Not a substitute for verified MRV or formal CBAM declarations.”

7) Board-ready use (suggested flow)

  1. Show €/t exported (commercial impact).
  2. Show €/year (strategy and cash exposure).
  3. Switch from Base → Stress (risk boundary).
  4. Ask: “Which lever is cheapest: reduce intensity, reduce EU share, or secure exemptions?”
  5. Export a PDF using Print / Save PDF for the board pack.
